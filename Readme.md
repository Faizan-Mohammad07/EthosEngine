We are building EthosEngine, a centralized SaaS platform designed to solve the biggest headache for companies in 2026: AI accountability and regulatory compliance. Instead of a standard chatbot, our project is a specialized "Agentic Governance" dashboard that acts as an automated Chief Ethics Officer. When a developer plugs their AI model into our system, we deploy a team of specialized IBM Granite-powered agents to stress-test it in real-time. These agents don't just look for bugs; they actively hunt for demographic bias, safety guardrail breaches, and "model drift" to ensure the AI remains fair and accurate. We are essentially building the "Security Camera and Health Inspector" that makes enterprise AI safe enough to be deployed in sensitive fields like finance, HR, and healthcare.

Technically, the system operates through a Multi-Agent Orchestration loop. We have three core agents—Fairness, Safety, and a Summarizer—that perform a "Recursive Validation" process. To solve the "Who audits the auditor?" problem, we use an adversarial debate logic where agents double-check each other's findings to ensure we aren't reporting false positives. The final output is a high-level "AI Nutrition Label" and a downloadable transparency report generated via IBM’s Carbon Design System. This turns complex, technical audit logs into a simple Red/Yellow/Green trust score that a CEO can understand at a glance. By leveraging watsonx.governance and the EU AI Act frameworks, we are providing a "Regulatory-as-a-Service" tool that helps teams move from prototype to production without the fear of legal or ethical "model rebellion.
